Non-newtonian fluids are very interesting types of fluids. They don’t behave in an intuitive way and it can be hard to predict how the will react under certain scenarios. A non-newtonain fluid is simply a fluid that does not follow Newton’s law of viscosity. This law states that a liquid’s viscosity will only change based on the amount of pressure and its temperature. An example is wate r when it is cooled into ice, the change in temperature changes its viscosity. Simila rly, if you threw something into water, a rock for example, the water would flow aroun d it. With a non-newtonian fluid, the higher pressure applied from the force of the roc k falling onto it could cause it to harden, becoming more like a solid and stopping th e rock. This would decelerate the rock and make it apply less pressure causing the no n-newtonian fluid to act like a liquid again and change viscosity, allowing the rock to fall through. Non-newtonian fluids are worth studying as they have several pract ical applications. Some types of clay for instance, or non-newtonian fluids and are used to help buildings resist earthquakes with their non-newtonian properties.
