Quantum mechanics is a branch of physics dealing with motion and interaction of subat omic particles. This incorporates the concepts of energy such as wave/particle duality, as well as the uncertainty principle, represented by the schrodinger's cat paradox where the cat is simultaneously dead and alive at the same time. This also includes the multiple dimensions and universes theory, called the many-worlds theory, where every single decision you make branches off into infinite amounts o f dimensions based on which decision you chose. Schrodinger's Cat and the Uncertainty Principle

This paradox involves a cat, poison, a geiger counter, radioactive material, and a hammer inside of a sealed container. The amount of radioactive material is minuscule enough that it only has a 50% chance of being detected over the course of an hour. If the geiger counter detects radiation, the hammer smashes the poison, killing the cat. This would mean that it is impossible to know whether the cat is dead or alive until someone opened the sealed container. This is called superposition in quantum physics, and works on the atomic level, and the Copenhagen interpretation as it is called, states that an object can exist in all possible configurations until it is observed, whence the entire system breaks down to a single configuration. This is a paradox as the cat can only be alive or dead in the real world, and cannot be both.
