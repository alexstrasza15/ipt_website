Northern lights are basically caused by the interaction of particles from the sun with the molecules in our atmosphere. It starts with the sun where particles of plasma are released from sunspots (areas or spots on the sun’s surface which are cooler than it’s surroundings, making it appear darker) into space known as solar wind. When the solar wind reaches the Earth, it is attracted to the north and south pole. This is why Aurora Borealis and Aurora Australis can only be seen in the north and south pole. As the plasma particles reach the atmosphere, their interaction with the molecules and particles in the atmosphere are what creates the amazing lights that entrance our population.
